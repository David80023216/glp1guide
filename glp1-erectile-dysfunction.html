GLP-1 Medications and Erectile Dysfunction: What Men Should Know

Do GLP-1 Drugs Cause or Improve ED?

One of the most common questions men have when starting a GLP-1 medication like Ozempic, Wegovy, or Mounjaro is whether it will affect their sexual health. The answer is encouraging: while you'll find conflicting reports online, the majority of research suggests GLP-1 medications may actually improve erectile dysfunction — not cause it.

Erectile dysfunction (ED) is extremely common among men with obesity and type 2 diabetes, the same populations most likely to be prescribed GLP-1 medications. This overlap makes it tricky to separate cause from effect, but emerging research is starting to provide clarity.

What the Research Shows

A meta-analysis presented at ENDO 2025 analyzed four studies with 396 participants who had type 2 diabetes and/or obesity. The findings were encouraging:

  • IIEF-5 scores improved — The International Index of Erectile Function (IIEF-5) is a standard questionnaire measuring ED severity. Men taking GLP-1 medications showed a mean improvement of 2.84 points compared to controls, indicating better erectile function. While this didn't reach statistical significance due to small sample sizes, the trend was clearly positive.
  • Testosterone levels increased modestly — Total testosterone rose from an average of 320 ng/dL to 368 ng/dL. This is meaningful because obesity-related low testosterone is common (affecting up to 40% of men with metabolic syndrome) and contributes to both ED and reduced libido.
  • SHBG levels showed improvements — Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) affects how much testosterone is bioavailable. Changes in SHBG suggest GLP-1s may improve the ratio of free (active) testosterone in the bloodstream.
Researcher insight: "ED is based on vascular health," explained Dr. Adnan Ezici of Boston Medical Center. "Animal studies show the GLP-1 molecule increases nitric oxide production and decreases endothelial inflammation in the vasculature. It is a possible mechanism to explain what is happening."

How GLP-1 Medications May Improve Erectile Function

The improvement in erectile function likely comes from multiple overlapping mechanisms:

1. Vascular Health and Nitric Oxide

Erections depend on healthy blood flow, which requires properly functioning blood vessels. GLP-1 medications improve endothelial function (the inner lining of blood vessels) and may increase nitric oxide production — the same molecule that ED drugs like Viagra and Cialis amplify. By improving vascular health, GLP-1s address a root cause of ED rather than just treating symptoms.

2. Weight Loss and Testosterone Recovery

Excess body fat converts testosterone to estrogen, lowering available testosterone and contributing to ED. Significant weight loss with GLP-1 medications can reverse this process. Studies show that losing just 10% of body weight through diet and exercise can raise testosterone by 55–115 ng/dL. GLP-1-assisted weight loss may achieve similar or greater effects.

3. Reduced Inflammation

Obesity creates chronic low-grade inflammation that damages blood vessels throughout the body, including those in the penis. GLP-1 medications reduce systemic inflammation, which may improve vascular function and blood flow to where it matters most.

4. Improved Insulin Sensitivity

Insulin resistance is a major contributor to ED in men with metabolic syndrome. By improving insulin sensitivity, GLP-1 medications may reduce the metabolic dysfunction that underlies both weight gain and sexual health problems.

What About Reports of ED Caused by GLP-1s?

Some men report new or worsening ED after starting GLP-1 medications, and a handful of studies have suggested a connection. Here's how to understand the conflicting data:

  • Small sample sizes — Most studies reporting ED as a side effect involved very few participants, making it hard to distinguish true drug effects from coincidence.
  • Confounding factors — Men prescribed GLP-1s typically have obesity, diabetes, or both — all independent risk factors for ED. The underlying condition, not the medication, may be the real cause.
  • Rapid weight loss effects — Rapid weight loss can temporarily affect hormone levels, sometimes causing a short-term dip in libido or sexual function before things stabilize and improve.
  • Nutritional changes — Reduced appetite can lead to nutritional deficiencies if not managed properly, and certain deficiencies (zinc, vitamin D) can affect testosterone production.
Bottom line: Most evidence points toward GLP-1 medications improving erectile function, not harming it. However, if you experience new or worsening ED after starting a GLP-1, talk to your doctor — it may be related to rapid weight loss, nutrition, or an underlying condition that needs attention.

The Muscle Mass Consideration

One genuine concern for men: GLP-1 medications can cause significant muscle loss alongside fat loss, with studies showing up to 39% of weight lost may be lean mass. For men with already low testosterone, this is particularly relevant:

  • Low testosterone already predisposes to muscle loss; GLP-1s may compound this
  • Muscle loss can reduce strength, exercise capacity, and overall physical confidence
  • Testosterone deficiency symptoms — low motivation, fatigue, low mood — can worsen with muscle loss
  • Combining GLP-1 therapy with resistance training and adequate protein intake is essential to preserve lean mass

Some experts suggest that men with symptomatic testosterone deficiency may benefit from testosterone therapy alongside GLP-1 medications, with the possibility of discontinuing testosterone after significant weight loss restores natural production.

Practical Steps for Men on GLP-1s

If you're a man taking a GLP-1 medication and want to protect or improve your sexual health:

  • Lift weights regularly — Resistance training preserves muscle mass and naturally boosts testosterone
  • Eat adequate protein — Aim for 0.8–1.0g of protein per pound of target body weight to support muscle retention
  • Get hormone levels checked — Ask for a full panel including total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, and estradiol before and during treatment
  • Monitor for improvements — Many men notice ED improvements gradually as weight decreases and vascular health improves
  • Discuss combination therapy — If you have confirmed low testosterone, ask your doctor whether adding testosterone therapy makes sense during your weight loss journey
  • Don't stop your GLP-1 — If you're experiencing ED, the medication is likely helping, not hurting. Work with your doctor to identify the real cause

The Bottom Line

The research on GLP-1 medications and male sexual health is still in its early stages, but the evidence so far is reassuring and even encouraging. GLP-1 drugs appear to improve the vascular, hormonal, and metabolic factors that underlie erectile dysfunction — likely making them beneficial for sexual health rather than harmful.

As larger clinical trials get underway, we'll have clearer answers about which men benefit most and whether GLP-1 medications might eventually be part of a comprehensive approach to men's sexual health. For now, if you're experiencing ED and are also a candidate for GLP-1 therapy, the weight loss, vascular improvements, and testosterone recovery these medications offer may address root causes rather than just symptoms.
